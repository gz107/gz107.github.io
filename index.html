About Me

Biography

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Power Engineering and Engineering Thermophysics at Xi'an Jiaotong University. My research focuses on adsorption thermodynamics, gas separation, and data-driven molecular simulation of porous materials.

I work at the interface of molecular modeling, machine learning, and energy systems, seeking to establish predictive structure–property relationships for adsorption-based technologies.

My work spans hydrogen storage in porous materials, carbon capture, and gas purification, with an emphasis on creating generalizable computational frameworks combining GCMC/MD simulations with machine learning surrogate models.

Research

Research Interests

Adsorption Thermodynamics & Kinetics

Microscopic adsorption mechanisms and isotherm representation in nanoporous materials.

Gas Separation & Purification

High-throughput screening of MOFs and COFs for industrial gas separation processes.

Hydrogen Storage

Porous material design for high-density, fast-response solid-state hydrogen storage.

Molecular Simulation

GCMC and MD simulations for structure–property relationships of adsorbents.

Machine Learning for Materials

Surrogate modeling and high-throughput material screening with data-driven methods.

PSA / TSA / ESA Processes

Multiphysics heat & mass transfer modeling in adsorption-based separation cycles.
