## Research Interests

### Adsorption Thermodynamics

Adsorption equilibrium, adsorption kinetics, isotherm modeling, and thermodynamic interpretation of gas adsorption in nanoporous materials.

### Molecular Simulation

Grand canonical Monte Carlo, molecular dynamics, first-principles calculation, and machine-learning potentials for adsorption and diffusion mechanisms.

### Porous Materials for Hydrogen

High-throughput screening and mechanism analysis of MOFs, COFs, zeolites, and related porous materials for hydrogen storage and purification.

### Gas Separation and Purification

Membrane separation, pressure swing adsorption, and process optimization for hydrogen recovery, carbon capture, and light-gas separation.

### Machine Learning for Materials Modeling

Data-driven surrogate models, interpretable machine learning, uncertainty quantification, and structure–property relationship analysis.

### Multiscale Energy-System Modeling

Coupling microscopic adsorption mechanisms with bed-scale transport, PSA/TSA/ESA processes, and hydrogen energy-system integration.

## Research Overview

My research aims to clarify how pore geometry, surface chemistry, framework flexibility, and thermodynamic operating conditions jointly determine gas adsorption and transport in nanoporous materials. To address this problem, I integrate:

- **Molecular simulation** to reveal microscopic adsorption and diffusion mechanisms.
- **Machine learning** to accelerate materials screening and property prediction.
- **Thermodynamic modeling** to represent adsorption equilibria and transferability across conditions.
- **Transport and process modeling** to evaluate material performance under realistic operating scenarios.

Ultimately, my goal is to develop generalizable modeling frameworks for adsorption-based hydrogen storage, hydrogen purification, carbon capture, and gas separation processes.
